Growth Reflection Questions

1. How did you use your own ideas to make art?

I used my own ideas when creating art by combining my blender and 3D rendering skills with the phtography skills I am learning in this class in order to make something unique and interesting.

2. What new techniques or processes did you learn?

I learned how to use new editing tecniques and software during this course. I had heard of darktable before this course, but had never actually used it. I also learned about code based processing filters like pixel sorting. I will be using the new editing skills I learned during this class in my 3D rendering workflow in order to get better results.

3. How is your artwork about who you are or what you like?

My artwork is less about a specific subject or idea, than it is about the process of photography. I find editing and processing photos interesting and fun, and I want my photos to show the fun that I had while making them.

4. What did you try that you weren't sure about?

Not really anything. I tried a few new things, like pixelsorting, but I wasnt worried cause there wasnt really any time constraint, and I knew it would work out in the end.

5. Did you engage with your peers for feedback?

No, not really. I got feedback during the friday showcase days, but that was after I was already done making the photo anyways.

6. How did you respond to challenges as you worked?

I faced a few challanges during this course with software not working the way I wanted it to, thankfully in all of these situations I was able to just switch to different software. (like with switching from lightroom to darktable because I didnt want to use adobe).

7. When did you step back and analyze your work during your projects?

Honestly I didnt very often, a lot of my work was made in short fast bursts, so there wasnt much time to reflect. Sometimes after I turn something in, I gain a new perspective on it, but it doesn't matter then because I already turned it in.

8. Did you use technology as a tool?

Yes, a lot. I spent signifigantly more time editing and tweaking photos after I took them than I spent actually taking photos. often the base photo would just be used as a texture in an entirely cg environment.
