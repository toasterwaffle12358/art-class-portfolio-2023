CAMERA SETTINGS

ISO

ISO controls the artificial brightness of the image. ISO changes how sensitive the sensor is to light. a higher iso gives a brighter image; however, because its based on the same amount of real light entering the camera, the image is more grainy.

Shutter Speed

Shutter speed controls the amount of time that the sensor is exposed to light. a longer shutter speed will give a brighter image, because more light reaches the sensor, but will also add more motion blur. a fast shutter speed will give less motion blur, but a darker image. it is not reccomended to use a shutter speed slower than 1/60s for handheld shooting.

Aperature

Aperature controls how open the lense is. aperature is expressed as an f-stop, and the range depends on what lense you are using. a higher f-stop gives a smaller hole and has more of the image in focus. a lower f-stop gives a brighter image, and less in focus. generally low aperatures are used in portraits as to blur the background, and high aperatures are used in landscapes to make sure everything is clear.
